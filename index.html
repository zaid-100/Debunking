A concession and rebuttal

As a muslim myself, i concede here that some ideas of the muslim faith may not be in sync with the ideas of western society, for example, the idea of gay marriage. However, this belief is centred around individuals and not as a law. The phrase "islamic law" or "sharia" in itself is meaningless unless the context is given. There is no absolute islamic law for society as there is no written document for "sharia" law. it doesn't exist; and what countries adopt in today's day are nit-picked modes of being described in the Quran which I and many other muslims like myself disagree with because the interpretation is subject to change.

Note: the phrase "sharia law" can be misleading in the pew news article , titled "The world's Muslims: Religion, Politics and Society", because it, itself, concedes that "Muslims who want to make sharia law the law of the country generally agree that requirements of islam should apply only to muslims."
Moreover, it says, "The survey also shows that Muslims differ widely in how they interpret certain aspects of islamic teachings." which means that whatever percentage of people agree with the imposition of "sharia law", the specifics are known only to the individual and for the individual. Most of the laws have nothing to do with society as a whole.
